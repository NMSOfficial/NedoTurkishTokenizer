Dear Editors,

We submit the manuscript “NedoTurkishTokenizer: A Morphology-Aware Tokenizer and Human-Adjudicated Benchmark for Turkish” for consideration in Language Resources and Evaluation.

The manuscript presents a self-contained morphology-aware tokenizer for Turkish and Final1000, a 1,000-item human-adjudicated benchmark for Turkish morphological tokenization. The final gold set contains 754 annotator-agreed items, 50 previously adjudicated items, and 196 newly adjudicated items, with 0 build issues. NedoTurkishTokenizer obtains boundary F1=0.6966 and is compared against Morpheus neural, XLM-R, BERTurk, mBERT, SentencePiece BPE/Unigram, character, and whole-word baselines. The paper also reports paired bootstrap significance tests, ablations, 10,000-text exact lossless round-trip validation, morphological probing, and a code-mixed foreign-root stress test.

We believe the manuscript fits LRE because it contributes a reusable language resource, a tokenizer tool, a benchmark, and a reproducible evaluation package for Turkish. The manuscript explicitly reports limitations, especially the weak code-mixed foreign-root result (F1=0.2597).

Before final submission, public GitHub, HuggingFace, Zenodo DOI, and arXiv links will be inserted into the availability statements.
